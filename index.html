One of the wonderful features of Indian cuisine or Indian food is the extremely wide range of choices that exist. They are all native to India and were created on the subcontinent. India is a nation with a great deal of diversity of soil types, climate and occupations and their cuisine varies in different parts of the country. There are also other factors that influence the cuisine such as spices, fruits, vegetables and herbs that can be easily found in each location.

The usual diet in India generally includes legumes, fruits, dairy products, eggs, meat, fish, honey, grains and vegetables. Over time, various regions of India became vegetarian due to the influence of Jainism and Hinduism. Indian food is actually known for the large number of spices that are included in their food. These are cardamom, coriander, garlic, ginger, turmeric and cumin. They have a mixture of spices called garam masala that is often used in Indian cuisine.

India is a country that has been heavily influenced by foreign invasions, trade and colonial occupation throughout its long history. All of these factors were influential in introducing new foods to the Indian people. For example, the Portuguese people brought the potato to the country a long time ago. They also introduced chilies and breadfruit. Indian cuisine is actually responsible for the burgeoning of trade between India and Europe. Along the so-called “Spice Route” they sent spices from India to places throughout Europe and much of Asia.
